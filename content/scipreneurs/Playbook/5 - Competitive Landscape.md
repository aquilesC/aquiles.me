Your biggest competitor isn't the lab across the hall: it’s “do nothing”.  
  
In academia, competition is a race for the "First to Publish." We look at citations, we track who is presenting at which conference, and we worry about being scooped by another research group.  
  
But for products, the "scoop" doesn't come from another scientist. It comes from the Status Quo.  
  
I see this mistake constantly with deep-tech spin-outs: Founders build a "Competitive Matrix" that only lists other companies and products with similar tech. They look at it and say, "Look, we are 20% more accurate than Startup X."  
  
In the real world, your customer isn’t comparing you to "Startup X." They are comparing you to the "Good Enough" workaround they’ve used for a decade.  
  
Your real competitors are:  
1️⃣ The Incumbent: The massive corporation that is "reliable" but slow.  
2️⃣ The Hack: The Excel sheet or manual protocol that "gets the job done."  
3️⃣ Indifference: The choice to do absolutely nothing because the "pain" of switching to your new tech is higher than the pain of the current problem.  
  
To win as a scipreneur, you don't just need to be better than the next paper. You need to be 10x better than the easiest way your customer can ignore you.  
  
And the hard part is understanding what “better” means. If you have been following this thread, probably you already found out or sense where this is going.
