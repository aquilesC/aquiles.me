Week 3.5 of the spinout journey: "What problem do you solve?" Is a very loaded question. Use it with care when defining your value proposition. 

Many people (especially at incubators) ask entrepreneurs to define the problem they are solving. Often for innovative technologies, it becomes impossible to formulate a statement without going in circles.  

Last time, I talked about our approach to measuring nanoparticle size. When asked about the problem we were solving, it was very easy to create a tautology: the problem people have is that they don't have our technology. Completely useless statement, but enough to go through a pitch competition. 

By focusing so much on solving a problem, I overlooked the importance of enabling something new for our users. Creating new knowledge or a deeper understanding of the systems they were studying. 

Creating value is not always about solving a defined problem. Innovative tech is often about enabling someone to do a job that was impossible to achieve before. 

If you don't know what problem you are solving, don't over stress. Focus, ALWAYS, on what value you create.
